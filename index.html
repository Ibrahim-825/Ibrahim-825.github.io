ENTRY NO. 01

Why You're My Hero

Before I knew what a hero looked like, I already had one.

You taught me how to ride a bike, how to tie my shoes, and — more importantly — how to get back up.

I used to think you knew everything. Now I think you just never let me see you figure it out.

Some heroes wear capes. Mine wears the same old flannel and gives the best hugs in the world.

ENTRY NO. 04

A Letter to You

Dear Dad,

I don't say this nearly enough, so today I'm putting it in writing: thank you.

Thank you for the early mornings and the late nights, for showing up even when it was hard, and for loving me in ways both loud and quiet. Thank you for teaching me how to be brave, how to be kind, and how to laugh at myself a little more.

I am who I am — in all the best ways — because you were there first, showing me how.

So here's to you, today and always. I love you more than words (or your jokes) could ever say.

— with love, always
